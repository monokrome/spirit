## The Western Esoteric Tradition

### Historical Development
1. **Greco-Egyptian** (1st-3rd c.): Hermeticism, Neoplatonism
2. **Medieval** (12th-15th c.): Alchemy, Kabbalah enters Europe
3. **Renaissance** (15th-17th c.): Ficino, Agrippa, Dee, Bruno
4. **Enlightenment** (18th c.): Freemasonry, Rosicrucians
5. **Modern** (19th-20th c.): Theosophy, Golden Dawn, Thelema
6. **Contemporary**: New Age synthesis, chaos magic

### Core Themes
- **Correspondence**: Microcosm mirrors macrocosm
- **Transformation**: Personal and spiritual evolution
- **Hidden Knowledge**: Esoteric wisdom beyond surface
- **Practice**: Theory applied through ritual and meditation

### Critical Thinking
These systems offer rich symbolic frameworks for self-exploration. Whether they describe objective metaphysical reality or serve as psychological tools remains a personal philosophical question. Use discernment while exploring.
