## Pyramid Research

### Acoustic Studies
Some researchers have measured acoustic properties of ancient structures:

- **Great Pyramid**: Various chambers produce different resonances
- **Malta Hypogeum**: Oracle chamber resonates around 110-111 Hz
- **Gothic Cathedrals**: Designed for acoustic effect

### Claims vs. Evidence
- Specific frequency claims (e.g., "432 Hz pyramid resonance") are speculative
- Actual acoustic measurements vary by location within structures
- Electromagnetic measurements remain controversial
- Most claimed frequencies come from channeling, not measurement

### Theory
Some propose Earth has an energy grid connecting sacred sites:
- **Ley lines**: Alfred Watkins (1921), originally alignment theory
- **Earth grid**: Various modern proposals (Becker-Hagens, etc.)
- **Vortex points**: Areas of concentrated energy

### Schumann Connection
The 7.83 Hz Schumann resonance is the one frequency with scientific basis—it's the fundamental electromagnetic resonance between Earth's surface and ionosphere.

## Historical Perspective

### Why These Sites?
Sacred sites often feature:
- Astronomical alignments (solstice, equinox)
- Geological features (springs, caves, mountains)
- Historic events (miracles, revelations)
- Accumulated practice (generations of worship)
- Aesthetic power (natural beauty, monumental architecture)

### Archaeology vs. Speculation
Mainstream archaeology explains most sites through:
- Human ingenuity without "lost technology"
- Astronomical knowledge developed over centuries
- Labor mobilization by organized societies
- Spiritual/ceremonial motivation

Alternative theories proposing advanced lost civilizations remain outside academic consensus.
